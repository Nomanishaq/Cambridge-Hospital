Academic Medical Centre



A health care facility that is often linked to a medical school and/or university complex and closely affiliated with or part of a degree-granting university. There are 120 to 380 academic medical centers in the US, depending on one’s definition.

Mission Teaching of medical students and physicians in training; research; tertiary patient care. Additional components, AMCs Schools for other health professions—nursing, pharmacy, dentistry, and allied health professions—and other clinical entities—faculty group practices, community health centres, nursing homes and, increasingly, community-based networks of practitioners.









Assessing Readiness for Accreditation



How do you know when you are ready for JCI academic medical center accreditation? This is an important question and one that should not be considered lightly. Accreditation is not a one-time accomplishment or “passing an examination.” JCI accreditation is an ongoing commitment to a process that demands time, financial resources, and follow-through, but will ultimately help you to improve continuously over time.





Introducing the Pathway to JCI Accreditation for Hospitals. This easy-to-navigate resource outlines the 10 steps hospitals and academic medical centers typically follow in their pursuit of accreditation. The milestones outlined within each step help your team organize activities and monitor progress throughout your accreditation preparation efforts.
